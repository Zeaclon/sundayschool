December 1–7: “The Vision of the Redemption of the Dead”

Scriptures: Doctrine and Covenants 137–138

These revelations, separated by 80+ years and 1,500 miles, both answer questions about life after death and testify of “the great and wonderful love made manifest by the Father and the Son” (D&C 138:3).

Key Points

  • D&C 137: Joseph Smith learned about the destiny of his brother Alvin, who died without baptism.
  • D&C 138: Joseph F. Smith, having lost family members, gained insight into the preaching of the gospel in the spirit world.
  • Both revelations show God’s plan for all His children and His desire for everyone to receive salvation.

Ideas for Learning at Home and Church

  • Read D&C 137; 138:30–37, 57–60 and consider God’s plan for salvation.
  • Discuss: Why does God require ordinances and covenants? How would you explain this to someone?
  • Reflect and write:
    • “Because of these revelations, I know that Heavenly Father …”
    • “Because of these revelations, I know that the Father’s plan of salvation …”
    • “Because of these revelations, I want to …”
  • Compare President Joseph F. Smith’s vision with other scriptures (1 Nephi 11:1–6, Joseph Smith—History 1:12–19) and personal revelation experiences.
  • Consider President Russell M. Nelson’s teaching: the Savior’s work continues on the other side of the veil (D&C 138:25–60).
